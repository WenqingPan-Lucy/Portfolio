Ocean Habitat

Project Overview

A series of interactive posters that immerse viewers in the stark realities of ocean pollution, inviting them to empathize with the silent struggles of marine life.

Artist Statement

My artistic pursuit is driven by a deep desire to illuminate the plight of sea creatures and the ecological damage wroughtbyhuman activity. Through a series of interactive pieces, I aim to engage viewers in a visceral exploration of these themes, weaving together aesthetic inspiration from both cinematic and real-world experiences.

Fishgroup


This interactive piece reflects the profound impact of human influence on marine life, featuring a school of ocean fish. As users click on the scene, the fish instinctively swim toward the selected position, circling it in a graceful dance. Yet, with each interaction, the number of fish steadily diminishes, poignantly symbolizing the shrinking populations of marine species under the relentless pressures of human activity

Jellyfish


The jellyfish poster offers a poignant reflection on the delicate balance of ocean ecosystems. With each user interaction, the jellyfish begin to wither and descend, their graceful forms fading away, symbolizing the insidious impact of pollution and climate change. This haunting imagery underscores the vulnerability of these ethereal creatures, reminding us of the fragile thread by which they—and countless others—are sustained

Coral


The coral piece invites users to interact with a vibrant underwater ecosystem. As the user clicks, the coral begins to grow, only to fade to white, symbolizing coral bleaching caused by rising ocean temperatures. This interaction aims to raise awareness about the detrimental effects of human-induced climate change on marine biodiversity.
